Overview

This report compares two popular generative models: GAN (Generative Adversarial Network) and DDPM (Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Model). Both models were trained to generate images, but they use fundamentally different approaches.

GAN (DCGAN) Adversarial

Two networks compete: a Generator creates fake images while a Discriminator tries to detect them. Through this adversarial process, the Generator learns to create realistic images.

  • Dataset: CIFAR-10 (Trucks)
  • Architecture: Generator + Discriminator
  • Training: 100 epochs

DDPM Diffusion

Learns to reverse a gradual noising process. Starting from pure noise, the model iteratively denoises the image step by step until a clear image emerges.

  • Dataset: CelebA (Faces)
  • Architecture: U-Net
  • Training: 60 epochs

Training Stability

GAN Training Challenging

GAN training is notoriously difficult due to the adversarial nature:

  • Mode Collapse Risk: Generator may produce only one type of image
  • Balance Required: G and D must be carefully balanced
  • Techniques Needed: Label smoothing, dropout, training control
Our Experience: V0 collapsed at epoch 91. Required 3 versions to stabilize.

DDPM Training Stable

Diffusion models are much easier to train:

  • No Collapse: Simple denoising objective
  • Stable Loss: Consistently decreases over time
  • No Balancing: Single network to train
Our Experience: Trained smoothly from epoch 1 to 60 with no issues.

Key Observation

While both models generate 64x64 images, the visual quality differs significantly. DDPM produces clearer facial features with better diversity, while GAN outputs are more abstract (partially due to the low-resolution CIFAR-10 dataset).

Pros & Cons

GAN

Advantages
  • Fast generation (instant)
  • Smaller model size
  • Well-studied architecture
  • Good for real-time apps
Disadvantages
  • Mode collapse risk
  • Training instability
  • Requires careful tuning
  • Limited diversity

DDPM

Advantages
  • Stable training
  • High image quality
  • Excellent diversity
  • No mode collapse
Disadvantages
  • Slow generation (~7 min)
  • Large model size
  • Long training time
  • High compute cost

Conclusion

Training Ease

DDPM Wins

Much more stable, no collapse issues

Image Quality

DDPM Wins

Clearer details, better diversity

Speed

GAN Wins

Instant vs 7 minutes per batch

Recommendation

  • Choose GAN if you need real-time generation or have limited compute resources
  • Choose DDPM if image quality and diversity are priorities and generation speed is not critical

Future Plans
